Beliefs

This list makes up my current operating system. It's based on experience, accumulated knowledge, and shifting perspective. These are less absolute truths and more a distillation of what gives me purpose & motivation and how I arrived at my mission.

  1. Economic growth is imperative for the long-term peace and prosperity of humanity. It creates better standards of living, longer lifespans, lower mortality rates, and more general fulfillment. Lack of economic growth may result in the opposite and has serious ramifications for the future of the population.1
  2. We must dare to create new things if we want economic growth that can advance humanity as a whole. Technology that leads to significant continuous productivity gains, wealth, and employment is an extremely important driver of economic growth. Progress made today can alter the trajectory of society tomorrow.
  3. We must have the courage work on hard valuable problems that move the needle on what's possible if we want meaningful economic growth that coincides with greater sustainability, happiness, and long-term vision for the future.
  4. We need to widen opportunity, mobility, and access to serve as many people. There is no limit to man’s creativity, therefore the more people that can build technology and build with technology, the more potential for economic growth there will be.
  5. We need diverse experiences, perspectives, skill sets, and interests to solve a wide range of problems. This can only add to a richer sphere of technological possibilities, more intelligent discourse, and more active participation from the population. Outcomes of economic growth only going to a concentrated minority limits the potential technology that can be created.
  6. We need tools to empower and promote genuine individuality. Individuality tied to a strong purpose enables man's greatest contribution. It helps connect people with their character, their gifts, their potential, and their art instead of strictly catering to the ego and obvious rewards which are ephemeral in nature and create skewed incentives. Such individuality can lead to greater economic, creative, and psychological freedom.
  7. The best leaders have a palpable energy about them that is infectious, confident, and mission-driven. They have real skill, inspiring vision, strong strategic decision-making, and relentless execution. They are focused on tangible progress and are focused on what they can give more than what they can get. They are driven by the long-term and bring others with them for the ride.
  8. Work ought to be challenging. We find deep meaning in doing our best, finding out what we're made of, and making things happen in spite of how difficult they may be, not in merely lounging in comfort. Business books and thought leaders say many things on how to develop organizational culture, but winning (sustainably, ethically, strategically at the right things) is the most important factor.
  9. Taste is the great equalizer. You can have the biggest war chest, most distinguished pedigree, most connected network, but there is still one thing none of those things can simply buy and that is taste. Taste is the combination of product smarts, technical capacity, and distribution strategy to manifest it to life. It may take time for the market and society at large to catch up to that taste, but eventually the score reveals itself.
  10. The future won’t design itself. Those who have vision and ability to create it have a moral obligation of doing so to create a better society. It also sets up the next generation of builders that can contribute to humanity's peace and prosperity. We have to look forward and actively build the future.

    We must build tomorrow today.

 I've seen firsthand what lack of economic growth looks like while growing up in the Philippines. It's better today, but there was significant income disparity, widespread corruption, underinvested infrastructure, and poor healthcare & education systems (unless you can afford it which is very expensive) reducing faith in the country's economic prospects in the future. This results in civil unrest and valuable human capital searching for better lives elsewhere, as was the case for my family.

In more extreme scenarios, declining economic growth leads to violence, crime, and war. This is why healthy economies are necessary. Without peace and prosperity, humans are focused strictly on survival and less on higher order things like human welfare, purpose, potential, imagination, and innovation. ↩
