Things I believe in

  • We are at the service of our users. A great product solves a real problem for real people in a very distinct way and helps them become tangibly empowered because of it.
  • Great products depend on stamina more than genius. We need to throw out a lot of good solutions in order to get to a great one. It's our duty to be persistent, patient, and relentless to figure out what that is and improve it over and over again.
  • Feedback is oxygen. Until what we're trying to build touches real people, all we have are a bunch of untested assumptions. Our goal is to learn how we can serve people better, not just to produce artifacts.
  • Culture is everything. A team's creative output and execution depends on the organizational infrastructure around it. The products we make are a direct reflection not just of our talents but of what we care about.
  • Conviction is the secret weapon. In our quest to provide a unique and differentiated supply for a market demand, a unified belief in what we're building and why we're doing it is our competitive advantage.
