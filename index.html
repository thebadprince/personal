Things I believe in

  • We are at the service of our users. A great product solves a real problem for real people and helps them become tangibly better after using it. If it doesn't provide clear value or improve their lives in a distinct way, it is not a great product.
  • Most of the things we'll make will suck. And that's okay. We need to churn out a lot of solutions in order to get to a great one. Not being fazed by failure and being comfortable with killing our darlings is part of the game.
  • Feedback is oxygen. Until what we're trying to build touches real people, all we have are a bunch of untested assumptions. Learning is the goal, not the artifact in itself. We win when we become more confident through results; we lose when we succumb to our biases.
  • Culture is everything. The best products have the organizational infrastructure behind it to propel creativity and execution. We can always do our part in how we interact with our peers and through the energy and enthusiasm we bring to the table.
  • Conviction is the secret weapon. Belief in what we're making is the nitrous fuel for building great things. In our quest to provide a great differentiated supply for the market demand, a unified mission becomes the competitive advantage.
