Jomi Cubol

Born in Manila, Philippines in 1990.

Goes to a technical school at age 10 to learn fundamentals in information technology, industrial drafting, electrical & mechanical engineering.

Moves to Yonkers, New York at age 14 where the creative arts becomes his emphasis: illustration, art direction, acting, dancing, and graphic & web design. Gets early freelance gigs for non-profits, aspiring musicians, and local organizations.

Goes to study English & Journalism in Albany, New York. Joins a fraternity and within the first year as a member, creates the national blog, successfully expands into a new university, and becomes its first public relations chairman.

After college, reads “Smart People Should Build Things” and becomes inspired to join a local startup building productivity tools for creative professionals.

In 2015, becomes a product designer working on consumer and enterprise software for the job search & HR industry.

In 2016, joins Condé Nast as a designer working on the Co/Lab team to pursue his personal mission in shifting the culture through media and publishing.

Outside of work, his interests lie in the intersection of art, technology, design, and metacognition.


—


The universal theme to his work and thinking is:

the pursuit of mastery and shared humanity.

As a lifelong and largely self-taught student, his insatiable curiosity and genuine love for learning has been instrumental to everything he has done so far and is yet to do. He believes doing things that are scary, risky, bold, and open for criticism & vulnerability is, in the words of George Lois, the ultimate adrenaline rush and ought to be driven to the precipice. After all, in this slow saunter towards death, the only thing that matters is how we make people feel.
