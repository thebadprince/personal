Library

This is an ongoing list of material that has created a significant impact on how I see the world and operate within it.

I don’t proclaim these texts are “must-reads” or that I agree with any of them completely (I don’t agree with anything completely, not even with myself). Only that these have communicated ideas and conjured emotions that I think about often, and make me discover something new everytime each time I read them again.

The order is based on how strongly the concepts have changed how I think about life, design, and business.

Books

  1. Zero to One by Peter Thiel
  2. Siddhartha by Hermann Hesse
  3. Breaking Smart by Venkatesh Rao
  4. Are We Human? by Beatriz Colomina and Mark Wigley
  5. Sagmeister & Walsh: Beauty
  6. What is a designer by Norman Potter
  7. Nichomachean Ethics by Aristotle
  8. Stubborn Attachments by Tyler Cowen
  9. Alchemy by Rory Sutherland
  10. White by Kenya Hara

Essays

  1. Applied Discovery by David Cole
  2. On The Importance of Being Ruthless by Jon Schlossberg
  3. Fundamental Truths by Rebekah Cox
  4. Tech as a Taste-Based Industry by Jong-Moon Kim
  5. You and Your Research by Richard Hamming
  6. Vital Elements of the Product Design Process by Ryan Singer
  7. Product Performances by Dustin Curtis
  8. Newspapers and Thinking the Unthinkable by Clay Shirky
  9. Climbing the Wrong Hill by Chris Dixon
  10. The Deployment Age by Jerry Neumann
